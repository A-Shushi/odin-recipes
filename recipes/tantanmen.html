Tantanmen Ramen

Home

Description

Ingredients

    Tare (Seasoning Base):
  • 2 tbsp Sesame paste (may substitute with peanut butter)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili oil, more or less to preference
    • Broth:
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups non-dairy milk (oat or soy milk works)
  • Ground pork mixture
    • Ground pork mixture:
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil, for frying
  • 285 g ground pork (may also use ground beef, turkey, chicken, or plant-based meats)
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated
  • 1 tbsp doubanjiang (spicy chili bean paste), more or less to preference
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
    • Toppings:
  • 2 servings ramen noodles
  • Leafy greens (bok choy, gai lan, or spinach)
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • Soft-boiled egg (you could also use ramen eggs, which should be prepared prior)
  • Chili oil, to taste

Directions

    Prepare the tare (seasoning base):
  1. Combine all the ingredients together for the tare and mix until smooth.
  2. Divide the mixture evenly into two bowls and set aside.
    3. Prepare the pork mixture:
  3. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add in the ground pork and cook until no longer pink, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
  4. Add in the ginger, garlic, and doubanjiang. Stir to coat the pork and saute until the aromatics are fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  5. Add in the mirin, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine and continue cooking until most of the liquid has evaporated, about 2 more minutes. Set aside and keep warm.
    6. Prepare the broth:
  6. In a sauce pot, combine together the chicken stock and non-dairy milk. Mix well.
  7. Bring to a low simmer, then cover and keep warm until ready to serve.
    8. Prepare the toppings:
  8. To a pot of boiling water, blanch your leafy greens for about one minute or until bright green and tender. Remove from the water, drain, and reserve for assembly.
  9. Bring the water to a boil again, and cook the ramen noodles according to packaged instructions until softened with a slight chew.
    10. To assemble:
  10. Divide the warmed broth between your two bowls. Mix well to evenly incorporate the tare with the broth.
  11. Add in a serving of ramen noodles to each bowl. Top with the ground pork mixture, blanched leafy greens, scallions, ramen egg, and a drizzle of chili oil, if desired. Serve immediately.
